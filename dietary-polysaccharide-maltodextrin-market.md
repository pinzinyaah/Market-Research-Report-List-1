<p><p>The dietary polysaccharide maltodextrin market is categorized by different grades based on dextrose equivalent (DE) values, primarily MD 10, MD 15, and MD 20. MD 10, with a lower DE, provides a mild sweetness and is often used in food products for bulking and stabilization. MD 15 offers a balance of sweetness and thickening properties, making it versatile in beverages and snacks. MD 20, having a higher DE, is utilized for its solubility and sweetness in sports drinks and energy products. Each type serves specific functional purposes in food formulation.</p></p>
