<p><p>The Front-Illuminated (FI) CMOS sensor market features two main types: Passive Pixel Sensor (PPS) and Active Pixel Sensor (APS) CMOS. PPS utilizes a simple structure with limited performance, making it cost-effective for low-resolution applications. In contrast, APS incorporates amplifying circuitry within each pixel, enhancing image quality and allowing for higher resolutions and faster readout speeds. This makes APS ideal for applications such as smartphones and digital cameras, driving its popularity in high-performance imaging markets.</p></p>
